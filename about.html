Why “IDIOT BOY”?

The name is tongue-in-cheek. It reflects how obvious bad behaviour can look in hindsight — a reminder that we all have blind spots. The goal isn’t to insult, but to invite people to do better.

Principles

  • Evidence-based: Nothing without sources or proof.
  • Legal and fair: No defamation, no hate speech, no personal attacks.
  • Playful: Humour is allowed — malice isn’t.
  • Truth comes first: We don’t post rumours — only what we can verify, prove, or clearly state as opinion.
  • Public interest, not public drama: Everything here exists to question power and improve the conversation — not stir gossip.
  • Everyone gets a say: Anyone mentioned has the right to respond — no ambushes, no ghosting.
  • Opinions, not accusations: We say what we think, not what we can’t prove — and we make that distinction clear.
  • Receipts or it didn’t happen: Every claim comes with context, evidence, or sources. If we can’t back it, it won’t go up.
  • We own our mistakes: If something’s wrong, we fix it fast, apologise, and move on transparently.
  • No hate, no harassment: Criticism is aimed at actions — never identities. Disrespect doesn’t belong here.
  • Globally aware, locally grounded: We post for everyone, but play by the laws and ethics where we live.
  • Ask, don’t assume: We question decisions, not people’s worth. Questions keep everyone honest — including us.
  • Learning as we go: Accountability is a process — we evolve as the facts do.
